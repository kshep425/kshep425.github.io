Where do you live?

I live in one of the top rated places to live in America: Columbia, MD in Howard County. Disclaimer: I grew up here so I'm very biased.

But a little about the history I know from the top of my head. Jim Rouse founded Columbia in the 60s with the merging of 3 farms on a napkin. He wanted it to be a place that would welcome people of all races, especially people with interracial marriages. So it's a very diverse community which I believed it was like that everywhere in America, but when I left for college out of the bubble I discovered everywhere is different from Columbia. It's a planned community, based on villages. Each village has a community center, pool, grocery store, and school. Then in the center of it all is Columbia Mall and the lake front. The lake front is a place for gathering and enjoying the weather. In the summer, they show music and host shows for free. It's very close to Merriweather Post Pavilion which hosts lots of great festivals and shows. It recently made the news because there was a chance Woodstock 50 would be playing there. It also recently had a huge makeover, if you ever get to tour the backstage do it (Spoiler alert: It has a pool for the artists and their guests). Another thing that's great about Columbia is how close it is to everything you could possibly need, with free parking! I really appreciate that free parking and convenience after living in the DC and Baltimore.
